The Chicago Bulls Dynasty Led By Michael Jordan

The Road to The Bulls' Six Championships

  • 1984 - Chicago Bulls draft Jordan with the third pick.
  • 1986 - Jordan announces the Bulls' arrival to the NBA by scoring 63 points in a playoff game against The Celtics led by Larry Bird.
  • 1987 - Bulls draft Horace Grant and trade for Sonics draft pick Scottie Pippen.
  • 1988 - Jordan wins league MVP for the first time.
  • 1989 - Bulls fire Doug Collins and hire Phil Jackson.
  • 1991 - Bulls overcome Bad Boy Pistons led by Isaiah Thomas in the Eastern Conference Finals
    and defeat The Los Angeles Lakers led by Ervin "Magic" Johnson to win first NBA championship for The Chicago Bulls.
  • 1992 - Bulls overcome Patrick Ewing led Knicks in the Eastern Conference Finals and faceoff against Clyde Drexler's Blazers in the finals.
    Jordan catches fire in Game 6 and ignites a come from behind win to capture a second championship for Chicago. This game highlights Jordon's icnonic "shrug".
  • 1993 - Bulls complete their first Three-peat against Barkley's Pheonix Suns.
  • 1994 - Jordan retires from basketball to pursue baseball career in honor of his late father.
  • 1995 - Jordon returns to the Bulls and leads them into the playoffs. They were ousted by a
    young Orlando Magic team led by Penny Hardaway and Shaquille O' Neal. In the offseason Dennis Rodman was acquired.
  • 1996 - The Bulls finish with a 72-10 record. They get their revenge on the Shaq and Penny led Magic, setting up their finals matchup against
    the Gary Payton and Shawn Kemp led Seattle SuperSonics. The Bulls beat the Sonics in six games and acquire their fourth champonship.
  • 1997 - Bulls capture fifth championship against John Stockton and Karl Malone led Utah Jazz. Jordan overcomes sickness in Game 5 which became known as the "Flu Game".
  • 1998 - The Chicago Bulls complete their second three peat as Jordon hits iconic basket known as "The Shot" to seal the win in Game 6 against The Utah Jazz.
